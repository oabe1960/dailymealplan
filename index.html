Sunday Sunday Jollof Rice & Fried Chicken

The post-church classic. Smoky party jollof with fried chicken and plantains (dodo).

üõí Sourcing

Costco: Rice (Parboiled), Chicken Drumsticks, Oil, Tomato Paste African Store: Scotch Bonnet (Ata Rodo), Thyme, Curry, Maggi

üìù Recipe

  1. Blend tomatoes, onions, and scotch bonnet peppers. Boil mixture until dry.
  2. Fry sliced onions in oil, add tomato paste and bay leaves. Fry for 5 mins.
  3. Add the boiled pepper mix, curry, thyme, Maggi, and meat stock. Cook for 15 mins.
  4. Wash rice thoroughly. Add to the pot. Cover with foil to trap steam ("Party Jollof" secret).
  5. Cook on low heat until soft. Serve with fried plantain and chicken.
üë∂ 3yo Tip: Jollof can be spicy. Mix a small portion of the tomato stew before adding the scotch bonnet for the toddler, or mix their rice with mixed veggies instead of spicy sauce.
Monday Boiled Yam & Garden Egg Stew

A great start to the week. Garden eggs (Igba) are nutritious and lighter than tomato stew.

üõí Sourcing

African Store: Puna Yam, Garden Eggs, Iru (Locust Beans), Palm Oil Walmart: Mackerel (Titus Fish), Onions

üìù Recipe

  1. Peel and cut yam into chunks. Boil with salt and a pinch of sugar until soft.
  2. Boil Garden Eggs until soft, then mash roughly.
  3. Bleach palm oil slightly, fry onions and locust beans (Iru).
  4. Add blended peppers (mild) and the mashed garden eggs. Add flaked smoked fish/mackerel.
  5. Simmer for 10 minutes.
üë∂ 3yo Tip: Mash the yam with a little butter for the toddler. Ensure all fish bones are removed meticulously.
